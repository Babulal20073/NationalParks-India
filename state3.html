Great Himalayan National Park

reat Himalayan National Park, situated in the state of Himachal Pradesh, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its pristine wilderness and biodiversity. Spanning over 1,171 square kilometers, this protected area is characterized by dense forests, alpine meadows, and towering snow-capped peaks. It harbors a diverse range of flora and fauna, including rare species such as the Himalayan musk deer and the elusive snow leopard.

Dachigam National Park

Dachigam National Park, located near Srinagar in Jammu and Kashmir, is renowned for its stunning natural beauty and rich biodiversity. Covering an area of approximately 141 square kilometers, it serves as a vital habitat for the endangered Hangul deer, also known as the Kashmir stag. The park's landscape is characterized by steep slopes, deep valleys, and dense forests of coniferous and deciduous trees. Visitors to Dachigam National Park can enjoy activities such as wildlife safaris, birdwatching, and trekking amidst the picturesque Himalayan surroundings.

Simlipal National Park

Simlipal National Park, situated in Odisha, India, is celebrated for its lush forests, diverse wildlife, and picturesque landscapes. Spanning over 2,750 square kilometers, it is one of the largest and oldest national parks in India. Simlipal is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including royal Bengal tigers, Asian elephants, and Indian bison. The park's rugged terrain, interspersed with rivers, waterfalls, and hills, offers visitors a captivating wilderness experience and opportunities for eco-tourism and wildlife safaris.

Bannerghatta National Park

Bannerghatta National Park, located near Bangalore in Karnataka, India, is renowned for its rich biodiversity and conservation efforts. Covering an area of approximately 104.27 square kilometers, it serves as a vital corridor for wildlife migration between the Eastern and Western Ghats. The park is home to a plethora of flora and fauna, including Asiatic lions, Bengal tigers, Indian elephants, and various species of birds and reptiles. Visitors can explore the park through safari rides, nature walks, and eco-tourism activities, offering an immersive experience in nature and wildlife conservation.

Panna National Park

Panna National Park, situated in Madhya Pradesh, India, is celebrated for its diverse ecosystems and stunning landscapes. Spanning over 542 square kilometers, it encompasses dense forests, cascading waterfalls, and winding rivers, providing a picturesque backdrop for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers. The park is renowned for its population of Bengal tigers, along with other iconic species such as leopards, deer, and sloth bears. Visitors can embark on thrilling safari experiences, birdwatching expeditions, and guided nature walks to immerse themselves in the park's natural beauty and wildlife diversity.

Nanda Devi National Park

Nanda Devi National Park, located in the Indian state of Uttarakhand, is renowned for its breathtaking mountain landscapes and rich biodiversity. It was established in 1982 and designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1988. The park is named after the Nanda Devi Peak, which is the second-highest mountain in India. Spanning an area of 630.33 square kilometers, it encompasses diverse ecosystems ranging from alpine meadows to dense forests.

Indravati National Park

Indravati National Park, situated in the Indian state of Chhattisgarh, is one of the finest wildlife reserves in Central India. Established in 1978, it covers an area of approximately 2799.08 square kilometers. Named after the Indravati River, the park is characterized by dense forests, rolling hills, and meandering streams. It is renowned for its rich biodiversity, including a diverse range of flora and fauna. The park is home to several endangered species such as the Indian bison (gaur), wild water buffalo, and hill myna. It offers visitors a chance to witness the beauty of the natural landscape and observe wildlife in their natural habitat

Betla National Park

Betla National Park, located in the state of Jharkhand, India, is one of the earliest national parks in India, established in 1986. It spans an area of about 979 square kilometers and is renowned for its rich biodiversity and scenic beauty. The park is home to diverse flora and fauna, including tigers, elephants, leopards, sloth bears, and a variety of bird species. Visitors can explore the park through jeep safaris, nature walks, and birdwatching excursions, offering an opportunity to immerse themselves in the wilderness and experience the natural wonders of Betla.

Bhitarkanika National Park

Bhitarkanika National Park, situated in Odisha, India, is renowned for its mangrove forests, diverse wildlife, and unique ecosystem. Spanning over 650 square kilometers, it is home to the endangered saltwater crocodile, along with numerous other species of reptiles, mammals, and birds. The park's mangrove ecosystem plays a crucial role in maintaining coastal biodiversity and protecting the region from natural disasters like cyclones and floods. Visitors can explore the park through boat safaris, witnessing the lush mangroves, abundant birdlife, and fascinating wildlife that call Bhitarkanika home.
