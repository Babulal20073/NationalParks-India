Dudhwa National Park

Dudhwa National Park, located in the foothills of the Himalayas in Uttar Pradesh, India, is renowned for its diverse flora and fauna. Spread over an area of approximately 490 square kilometers, it encompasses lush forests, grasslands, and marshes. The park is known for its population of rare and endangered species, including the Bengal tiger, Indian rhinoceros, and swamp deer (barasingha).

Kasu Brahmananda Reddy National Park

Kasu Brahmananda Reddy National Park, located in Hyderabad, Telangana, is a serene oasis amidst the urban landscape. Spanning over 390 acres, it offers lush greenery, rocky terrain, and a diverse range of flora and fauna. The park is named after Kasu Brahmananda Reddy, a former Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh, and is a popular destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts.

Guindy National Park

Guindy National Park, situated in Chennai, Tamil Nadu, is one of the smallest national parks in India, covering an area of about 2.70 square kilometers. Despite its small size, it holds significant ecological importance as it serves as a crucial green lung for the city. The park is renowned for its rich biodiversity, housing various species of plants, animals, and birds within its boundaries.

Khangchendzonga National Park

Khangchendzonga National Park, located in the Indian state of Sikkim, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its stunning biodiversity and unique cultural significance. Spanning over 1,400 square kilometers, it encompasses a diverse range of ecosystems, including glaciers, high-altitude lakes, and temperate forests. The park is named after Mount Khangchendzonga, the third highest peak in the world, which is revered by the indigenous communities as a sacred mountain.

Desert National Park

Desert National Park, located in the Indian state of Rajasthan, is a vast expanse of arid land encompassing approximately 3,162 square kilometers. Established in 1980, the park is renowned for its unique desert ecosystem and diverse wildlife. The landscape of Desert National Park is characterized by extensive sand dunes, rocky terrain, and scrub vegetation, typical of the Thar Desert.

Bhitarkanika National Park

Bhitarkanika National Park, situated in the Kendrapara district of Odisha, India, is a significant biodiversity hotspot renowned for its mangrove forests and estuarine crocodile population. Spanning over 650 square kilometers, it was established in 1998 and designated as a Ramsar Wetland of International Importance in 2002. The park's mangrove ecosystem serves as a vital habitat for a diverse array of flora and fauna, including several species of mangrove trees, plants, and aquatic organisms.

Ntangki National Park

Ntangki National Park, located in Peren district of Nagaland, India, is known for its dense forests and rich biodiversity. Established in 1993, it covers an area of approximately 202 square kilometers. The park is characterized by its hilly terrain, interspersed with valleys, streams, and waterfalls, offering scenic landscapes for visitors to explore. Home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, Ntangki National Park is renowned for its population of rare and endemic species.

Murlen National Park

Murlen National Park, situated in the Champhai district of Mizoram, India, is renowned for its pristine wilderness and unique biodiversity. Established in 1991, it covers an area of approximately 200 square kilometers and is characterized by its rugged terrain, dense forests, and steep slopes. The park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including several rare and endemic species.

Balphakram National Park

Balphakram National Park, located in the state of Meghalaya, India, is a biodiverse sanctuary known for its stunning landscapes and unique flora and fauna. Spread across an area of approximately 220 square kilometers, it encompasses rugged terrain, deep gorges, and dense forests, offering visitors a glimpse into the unspoiled wilderness of the region. The park is renowned for its rich biodiversity, housing a variety of plant species, including rare orchids, medicinal herbs, and endemic flora.
