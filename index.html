Explore National Parks

Jim Corbett National Park

Nestled in Uttarakhand's Himalayan foothills, Jim Corbett National Park, named after conservationist Jim Corbett, is a wildlife haven. Home to Bengal tigers, leopards, elephants, and 600 bird species, it offers thrilling safaris. Its diverse landscapes, including forests and rivers, provide an unforgettable nature experience. With its rich biodiversity and stunning scenery, Jim Corbett National Park promises an adventure of a lifetime.

Kaziranga National Park

Kaziranga National Park in Assam, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its one-horned rhinoceros population. Covering over 430 square kilometers, it's home to diverse wildlife, including tigers and elephants. Its varied ecosystems, from grasslands to wetlands, support rich biodiversity. Kaziranga's conservation efforts make it a crucial habitat for endangered species.

Ranthambore National Park

Ranthambore National Park in Rajasthan, India, is famed for its Bengal tigers. Covering 392 square kilometers, it boasts diverse landscapes of forests and lakes. Besides tigers, the park shelters leopards and sloth bears. Its historical Ranthambore Fort adds cultural appeal. Conservation efforts prioritize wildlife and heritage protection.

Sunderbans National Park

The Sunderbans National Park in West Bengal, India, is the world's largest mangrove forest, spanning India and Bangladesh. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is renowned for its Royal Bengal tigers and diverse ecosystem. Boat safaris offer a glimpse of its intricate water channels and lush greenery. The park's preservation is vital for protecting endangered species and maintaining ecological balance.

Kanha National Park

Kanha National Park, in Madhya Pradesh, India, is renowned for its Bengal tiger population and diverse wildlife. Its dense forests and expansive grasslands offer a stunning backdrop for safaris and nature walks. Conservation efforts focus on preserving the park's natural habitat and protecting endangered species. Visitors can enjoy the park's biodiversity while supporting conservation initiatives.

Bandhavgarh National Park

Bandhavgarh National Park in Madhya Pradesh, India, is renowned for its Bengal tiger population and diverse wildlife. The park's rugged terrain and dense forests create a haven for various species, including leopards, deer, and birds. Conservation initiatives focus on preserving this rich biodiversity and fragile ecosystem.

Gir National Park

Gir National Park in Gujarat, India, is renowned as the last refuge of the Asiatic lion. Covering 1,400 square kilometers, it's home to various wildlife species like leopards, deer, and crocodiles. The park's diverse landscape includes deciduous forests, grasslands, and rocky hills. Conservation efforts focus on safeguarding the endangered Asiatic lions and preserving their habitat. Gir offers thrilling safari experiences for visitors to observe these magnificent creatures up close in their natural habitat.

Periyar National Park

Periyar National Park in Kerala, India, boasts stunning natural beauty and rich biodiversity. Spanning 925 square kilometers, it surrounds the picturesque Periyar Lake, formed by the Mullaperiyar Dam. The park is renowned for its diverse wildlife, including elephants, tigers, leopards, sambar deer, and gaurs. Bird enthusiasts flock here to spot over 260 bird species. Boat cruises on Periyar Lake provide a tranquil way to observe the wildlife.

Tadoba National Park

Tadoba National Park in Maharashtra, India, is renowned for its tiger population and diverse wildlife. Spanning 625 square kilometers, it offers rich biodiversity and dense forests, making it a haven for nature lovers. Visitors can embark on jungle safaris to spot tigers, leopards, sloth bears, and various bird species in their natural habitat. Its scenic landscapes and diverse flora make it a popular destination for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers alike.
