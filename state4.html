Sanjay Gandhi National Park

Sanjay Gandhi National Park, located in Mumbai, India, is a vast protected area renowned for its biodiversity and scenic beauty. Covering over 100 square kilometers, it encompasses dense forests, rocky cliffs, and serene lakes. The park is home to a variety of wildlife, including leopards, deer, macaques, and numerous bird species. One of its prominent attractions is the Kanheri Caves, ancient rock-cut Buddhist caves dating back to the 1st century BCE. Visitors can enjoy nature trails, wildlife safaris, and explore the cultural heritage of the Kanheri Caves while experiencing the tranquility of this urban oasis.

Rajaji National Park

Rajaji National Park, located in the Indian state of Uttarakhand, is a sprawling wildlife reserve known for its diverse flora and fauna. Named after the renowned freedom fighter C. Rajagopalachari, the park spans across the Shivalik ranges and offers a picturesque landscape of dense forests, grasslands, and river valleys. It is home to a rich variety of wildlife, including Asian elephants, tigers, leopards, deer, and over 400 bird species.

Kalakad Mundanthurai Tiger Reserve

Kalakad Mundanthurai Tiger Reserve, situated in the southern Indian state of Tamil Nadu, is one of the largest protected areas in the region. Named after the Kalakad and Mundanthurai mountain ranges, it encompasses diverse ecosystems ranging from tropical wet evergreen forests to dry deciduous forests. The reserve is renowned for its rich biodiversity, including a variety of plant species, mammals, reptiles, and birds. As a vital habitat for the endangered Bengal tiger, it plays a crucial role in tiger conservation efforts in India.

Sultanpur National Park

Sultanpur National Park, located in the Gurgaon district of Haryana, India, is a popular bird sanctuary renowned for its avian diversity. Spread over an area of approximately 143 acres, the park provides a haven for migratory birds, particularly during the winter months. It offers a serene environment with various wetland habitats, including marshes, lakes, and grasslands, attracting thousands of bird species such as Siberian cranes, greater flamingos, and painted storks. Apart from birdwatching, visitors can enjoy nature trails, bird hides, and observation towers for a close encounter with the park's rich biodiversity.

Campbell Bay National Park

Campbell Bay National Park is situated in the Nicobar district of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, India. Covering an area of approximately 426 square kilometers, this national park encompasses a diverse range of habitats including tropical evergreen forests, mangroves, and marine ecosystems. It is renowned for its rich biodiversity, housing various endemic and endangered species such as the Nicobar megapode, Nicobar pigeon, and Nicobar scrubfowl. The park also features pristine beaches, coral reefs, and scenic viewpoints, offering visitors opportunities for hiking, birdwatching, and snorkeling amidst stunning natural surroundings.

Sri Venkateswara National Park

Sri Venkateswara National Park, located in the Chittoor and Cuddapah districts of Andhra Pradesh, India, is renowned for its scenic beauty and biodiversity. Spanning an area of about 353 square kilometers, the park is nestled in the Eastern Ghats range, offering spectacular landscapes of lush forests, rolling hills, and cascading waterfalls. It is named after the famous temple of Lord Venkateswara (also known as Balaji) situated atop the Tirumala Hills within the park boundaries.

Mollem National Park

Mollem National Park, situated in the Indian state of Goa, is a biodiverse sanctuary known for its rich flora and fauna. Spanning approximately 240 square kilometers, it encompasses lush tropical forests, rolling hills, and sparkling waterfalls, creating a haven for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts. The park is home to a variety of wildlife species, including Bengal tigers, leopards, Indian bison (gaur), sloth bears, and various species of deer and monkeys.

Blackbuck National Park

Blackbuck National Park, Velavadar, located in Gujarat, India, is renowned for its conservation efforts focused on the endangered blackbuck antelope. Spanning over 34 square kilometers, it provides a protected habitat for these graceful creatures, along with various bird species and other wildlife. The park's unique grassland ecosystem, dominated by Prosopis and Butea shrubs, offers a picturesque landscape for visitors to explore.

Marine National Park

Marine National Park, situated in the Gulf of Kutch in Gujarat, India, is the country's first marine sanctuary. Covering an area of around 458 square kilometers, it is renowned for its rich marine biodiversity and vibrant coral reefs. This park is home to various marine species, including coral reefs, dolphins, dugongs, and a wide array of fish and invertebrates.
